# Wide-Open Brainstorm Prompt Template

Run a wide-open, multi-round, multi-perspective brainstorming room for [PRODUCT / IDEA / SYSTEM].

The goal is not a narrow feature list. The goal is to think seriously, playfully, tactically, strategically, practically, whimsically, and a little absurdly about what this could become for [USER / AUDIENCE].

Think at multiple altitudes and move fluidly between them:

- **10,000 feet:** goals, strategy, ecosystem, health, drift, long-term direction, what matters.
- **1,000 feet:** projects, workstreams, groups, documents, tickets, branches, user journeys, operational patterns.
- **Ground level:** exact states, actions, statuses, buttons, labels, recovery flows, next clicks.

Do not stay at one altitude. Explore how the system can smoothly zoom out, zoom in, and jump with precision to exactly the right place at the right time.

Pull the best ideas from existing tools and patterns, but assume the current tools are too flat, tactical, or mechanical. Invent the awesome version: a control panel, command center, cartographic observatory, captain's bridge, workshop, garden, lab, cockpit, or whatever metaphor helps the human understand and act.

Include serious and actionable ideas, but also whimsical and silly organizing metaphors. The whimsy should make complex state easier to perceive, not merely decorate it.


Also run a **time-horizon leapfrog pass**. Ask:

- **6 months out:** What would the smart, competent solution look like once obvious rough edges are fixed?
- **12 months out:** What would smart teams converge on after the pattern becomes legible and early tooling exists?
- **24 months out:** What would the mature, integrated version assume as table stakes?
- **Leapfrog:** What bypasses those predictable smart ideas and arrives closer to **THE FUTURE** now?

For each horizon, separate obvious next improvements from deeper shifts enabled by memory, usage data, trust, integrations, better mental models, or changed user expectations. Then identify the brave move that feels premature today but may become obvious later. Use the future pass to avoid local maxima: preserve near-term practicality while naming which ideas leapfrog the expected market path.

Run multiple rounds internally:

1. **Raw divergence:** generate a huge range of ideas without judging too early.
2. **Yes-and:** build on the strangest and strongest ideas; combine metaphors with practical workflows.
3. **Grounding:** identify which ideas actually help the human know what to do next.
4. **Altitude pass:** ensure support for big-picture strategy, group/project review, and tactical action.
5. **Distillation:** extract the most differentiated concepts and the product thesis.
6. **Future leapfrog:** compare 6/12/24-month expected-smart solutions against the moves that get closer to THE FUTURE now.

Emphasize:

- what state the human needs to understand;
- why each item/workstream/session/user/action exists;
- grouping by goals and meaning, not just by source/tool/file;
- hotspots, neglected areas, duplication, collisions, stalled fronts, and attention debt;
- actions that help the user recover, resume, summarize, archive, coalesce, hand off, or finish work;
- optional AI/LLM-backed synthesis, grouping, risk detection, memory, and next-action recommendations;
- what is missing from existing tools because they are too tactical or tool-centric.

Output:

1. North Star / product thesis
2. What existing tools or current approaches miss
3. Big ideas from practical to wild
4. Views and organizing metaphors
5. Detection/status/intelligence ideas
6. Actions and workflows
7. AI-backed synthesis or automation ideas
8. Whimsical delight ideas
9. Top differentiated ideas
10. Time horizons: smart solution in 6 months, 12 months, and 24 months
11. Leapfrog ideas: what reaches THE FUTURE before the obvious roadmap
12. What to build first vs. what to dream about

Tone: smart, wide-open, serious enough to be useful, silly enough to find non-obvious ideas, and centered on helping the human move smoothly between big-picture understanding and precise tactical action.
